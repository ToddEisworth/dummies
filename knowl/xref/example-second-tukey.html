Example 1.5.6.

Continuing with the notation of Example [cross-reference to target(s) "ex:prodseq" missing or not unique], let us now make the additional assumption that the cardinality of \(A\) is strictly less than each \(\lambda(a)\) and then define \(B\) to be the range \(\{\lambda(a):a\in A\}\) of \(\ov{\lambda}\) (considered as a function from \(A\) onto \(B\)). Our aim is to show that \(\prod f\) and \(\prod B\) are essentially the same, in the sense that for any ideal \(I\) on \(A\) there is a corresponding ideal \(J\) on \(B\) such that \((\prod f, \leq_I)\) and \((\prod B, \leq_J)\) are Tukey equivalent, and vice versa.
We define \(T\) to be the set of functions in \(\prod S(a)\) that are constant on the pre-images of elements of \(B\) under \(\ov{\lambda}\text{.}\) Thus, a function in \(\prod f\) gets into \(T\) if its value at \(a\) depends only on the value \(\lambda(a)\text{.}\) Our assumption that \(|A|\) is less than \(\min(B)\) is used to show that \(T\) is cofinal in \(\prod f\text{:}\) Given \(g\in\prod f\) we define \(g^*\in T\) by setting
\begin{equation*} g^*(a) = \sup\{g(b):\lambda(a)=\lambda(b)\}. \end{equation*}
It is clear that \(g\leq g^*(a)\text{,}\) and \(\lambda(a)=\lambda(b)\) implies \(g^*(a)=g^*(b)\text{,}\) so \(g^*\) will be in \(T\) provided we can show \(g^*\) is in \(\prod f\text{.}\) But this is where are assumption \(|A|<\min(B)\) comes to the rescue, as in each coordinate \(a\) the set \(\{g(b):\lambda(a)=\lambda(b)\}\) will be bounded below \(\lambda(a)\text{.}\) The structure \(\prod B\) is isomorphic to \(T\) in a natural way as well, for given \(x\in \prod B\) define \(\psi(x)\in T\) through the formula
\begin{equation*} \label{eq:psiform} \psi(x)(a)= x(\lambda(a)). \end{equation*}
Thus, \((\prod f, \leq)\) and \((\prod B, \leq)\) are Tukey equivalent by way of Corollary [cross-reference to target(s) "cor:equivcofinal" missing or not unique].
Much more can be said, though, as the function \(\ov{\lambda}\) induces a natural correspondence between ideals on \(A\) and ideals on \(B\text{:}\) given an ideal \(I\) on \(A\) (respectively, \(J\) on \(B\)) then we get another ideal \(J\) on \(B\) (respectively, \(I\) on \(A\)) by defining
\begin{equation*} X\in J\Longleftrightarrow \{a\in A:\lambda(a)\in X\}\in I, \end{equation*}
and we denote this relationship by writing \(J=\ov{\lambda}[I]\text{.}\)
We now show that if \(J = \ov{\lambda}[I]\text{,}\) then the function \(\psi\) defined above is an isomorphism between \((\prod B, \leq_J)\) and \((T,\leq_I)\text{.}\) To see this, note that for \(x\) and \(y\) in \(\prod B\) we can apply [cross-reference to target(s) "eq:psiform" missing or not unique] to obtain the equalities
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \{a\in A:\psi(x)(a)<\psi(y)(a)\}&=\{a\in A: x(\lambda(a))<y(\lambda(a)\}\\&=\ov{\lambda}^{-1}(\{\theta\in B: x(\theta)<y(\theta)\}). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Since \(J=\ov{\lambda}[I]\text{,}\) this shows us that \(x\leq_J y\) in \(\prod B\) if and only if \(\psi(x)\leq_I \psi(y)\) in \(\prod f\text{.}\) We conclude that for every ideal \(I\) on \(A\) there is an ideal \(J\) on \(B\) such that \((\prod f, \leq_I)\) is Tukey equivalent to \((\prod B, \leq_J)\text{,}\) and vice versa. \(\blacksquare\)
